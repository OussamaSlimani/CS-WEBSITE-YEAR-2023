IEEE Computer Society Chapter ENIS Student Branch.

The IEEE Computer Society is renowned for promoting computer science and engineering. The Enis Student Branch Chapter, established on January 27, 2010, at the National School of Engineering of Sfax, focuses on providing its members with essential Information Technology knowledge to boost their careers and improve their skills. Through engaging programs and networking opportunities, the chapter empowers aspiring professionals to excel in the ever-evolving technology landscape.

IEEE CS ENIS SBC MAIN GOALS

Empowering Minds, Building Innovators.

Event Organisation

We organize a diverse range of activities to fulfill our chapter's mission and cater to the needs of our members. These activities include conferences, competitions, hackathons, entertainment events, and workshops. We believe in actively involving our members in the organizational aspects of our work to ensure a sense of ownership and inclusivity within the chapter.

Technical and Communicational Prowess

By creating various sustainable and collaborative opportunities to engage in, we ensure that those who are deeply involved with our chapter’s work can truly understand what it means to be an engineer. Our members have ample occasions to develop their hard and soft skills. Our chapter takes pride in nursing the potential of all its members.

Community Building

Our members actively interact and engage in various debates, fostering a creative and collaborative environment where they can freely express and share numerous innovative ideas. To strengthen our team-work skills, we frequently conduct online meetings to accommodate the requests of our members. We prioritise growing friendships as well as competitiveness amongst our members.

TechXchange 2021

TechXchange is an event proposed by the IEEE CS SYP that focuses on emerging technologies in the field of Computer Science. In 2021 and in collaboration with IEEE IAS ENIS SBC, the IEEE ENIS CS SBC hosted TechXchange while pairing it with the Enis's Industrial Forum's competition. The workshops that were hosted focused on IoT, embedded systems, Artificial Intelligence, Entrepreneurship and much more. Many industrial leaders and professionals have come forward to share their experiences with the participants and to provide insight to their respective fields of work. This provided students with ample opportunities to learn what it means to be an engineer in a professional setting while also reinforcing their technical prowess.

Hello World! 2.0

Hello World 2.0 stands as a captivating and dynamic Problem-Solving event, expertly crafted and hosted by the prestigious IEEE ENIS Computer Society Student Chapter. In its eagerly anticipated Second edition, Hello World will be divided into two enthralling segments. The first part will feature comprehensive training sessions focusing on algorithms and diverse data structures, commencing on the 23rd of April 2022. These sessions aim to equip participants with essential problem-solving skills and technical knowledge. The excitement continues with the second segment, a thrilling Problem Solving competition, scheduled to take place on the 14th of May 2022 at the esteemed National Engineering School of Sfax (ENIS). During this intense competition, participants will put their newfound skills and ingenuity to the test, engaging in challenging problem-solving scenarios. Hello World 2.0 embodies a unique opportunity for aspiring minds to delve into the captivating world of problem-solving and computer science. It promises to be an extraordinary experience that fosters growth, creativity, and camaraderie among participants, leaving an indelible mark on their journey of intellectual discovery.
