TechXchange 2021

TechXchange is an event proposed by the IEEE CS SYP that focuses on emerging technologies in the field of Computer Science. In 2021 and in collaboration with IEEE IAS ENIS SBC, the IEEE ENIS CS SBC hosted TechXchange while pairing it with the Enis's Industrial Forum's competition. The workshops that were hosted focused on IoT, embedded systems, Artificial Intelligence, Entrepreneurship and much more. Many industrial leaders and professionals have come forward to share their experiences with the participants and to provide insight to their respective fields of work. This provided students with ample opportunities to learn what it means to be an engineer in a professional setting while also reinforcing their technical prowess.

Hello World! 2.0

Hello World 2.0 stands as a captivating and dynamic Problem-Solving event, expertly crafted and hosted by the prestigious IEEE ENIS Computer Society Student Chapter. In its eagerly anticipated Second edition, Hello World will be divided into two enthralling segments. The first part will feature comprehensive training sessions focusing on algorithms and diverse data structures, commencing on the 23rd of April 2022. These sessions aim to equip participants with essential problem-solving skills and technical knowledge. The excitement continues with the second segment, a thrilling Problem Solving competition, scheduled to take place on the 14th of May 2022 at the esteemed National Engineering School of Sfax (ENIS). During this intense competition, participants will put their newfound skills and ingenuity to the test, engaging in challenging problem-solving scenarios. Hello World 2.0 embodies a unique opportunity for aspiring minds to delve into the captivating world of problem-solving and computer science. It promises to be an extraordinary experience that fosters growth, creativity, and camaraderie among participants, leaving an indelible mark on their journey of intellectual discovery.

IEEEXtreme 16.0

In order to participate in IEEEXtreme 16.0, an international challenge, IEEE student members worldwide competed for 24 hours to solve programming problems. IEEE Computer Society ENIS Student Chapter brought together members on October 21 and 22 at the national engineering school of Sfax. Attendees enjoyed the fantastic environment and various activities planned for them. Once the problem set went online, everyone dove into their laptops with full concentration. We thank the 100 students who participated and made ENIS proud with their performance and spirit. Challenges demanded creativity, logic, and determination. For 24 hours, participants were immersed in coding, debugging, and intense discussions. IEEEXtreme 2023 was a success, and the organizers look forward to the next edition. Let the brilliance of the participants shine in the realm of IEEEXtreme!

Hour of Code

The IEEE Computer Society ENIS SB orchestrated a transformative event, "Hour of Code," on December 4th, 2022, with the noble mission of equipping children with the foundational principles of coding and inspiring them to explore the fascinating realm of computer science. Through this interactive initiative, young minds were introduced to the magic of programming, fostering a sense of curiosity and enthusiasm for technology. By encouraging learning and discovery, the event not only opened doors to endless possibilities but also nurtured the future innovators and problem solvers who will undoubtedly shape the world of tomorrow.

A big, heartfelt expression of gratitude goes out to each and every one of you for your active participation in our recent PHP workshop. Your eagerness to learn and your determination to acquire new coding skills made the workshop an unforgettable and special experience for us. We are immensely grateful to be surrounded by such a passionate and knowledge-hungry group of learners. Git/GitHub Workshop

In collaboration with the IEEE Women In Engineering ENIS Student Affinity Group and in an initiative to further instill Women's Engineering capacity, the IEEE ENIS Computer Society has organized a Git and Github Workshop. As part of any professional workplace in the IT field, mastering project version control is vital to the integrity of any large scale endeavor. As such, students are expected to be mindful of the functionalities of Git and Github to easily integrate their work into the pre-existing project ecosystem. Having invited the data engineer and former CS Chairman, Mr Kadhem Belghuith, to host the workshop, students have managed to grasp the fundamentals of Git and Github while also benefiting from his experience in project management using different kinds of tools.

C++ Traning Session

In Preparation to the third version of Hello World!, the IEEE ENIS Computer Society has solicited the help of a renowned problem solving coach. Doctor Atef Masmoudi, a famous man whose success has placed him in the most prestigious universities, has agreed to host 2 Problem Solving Workshops that took place on the 14th and 16th of April online. With the goal of teaching students about data structures, dynamic programming and other different paradigms, our coach has spared no effort into helping participants grasp hard concepts and form an understanding on how to tackle any problem. Students who joined the workshops have had much to learn and with Doctor Atef's help, were able to get acquainted with the roadmap to master programming and to kickstart their passion for Problem Solving.

HELLO WORLD! 3.0

The CS Junior workshop, organized by CS ENIS IEEE SBC, proved to be a wonderful experience On May 10th, our team visited the Primary School Habib 2, where children were taught about logic gates, electronics and computer science as a whole. Making a positive impact on their lives was a rewarding experience. The main goal was to empower the next generation of thinkers, achieved through an interactive website that made learning enjoyable. Our contribution to our SB's ingenious and very own IEEE Junior aimed to create a brighter future through education and to foster ambition in the younger generation.

HELLO WORLD! 3.0

The epic quest of problem-solving and coding excellence, "Hello World! 3.0," concluded at the Digital Research Centre of Sfax. Participants faced thrilling challenges, pushing coding skills to the limit. The competition showcased talent and fostered camaraderie. Enthusiastic coders worked side by side, exchanging ideas, and learning from one another. Anticipation was high as participants envisioned moments of triumph. Throughout the day, they experienced the rush of completing challenges and the satisfaction of overcoming hurdles. "Hello World! 3.0" united people with a common passion for programming. It celebrated the art and science of coding, fostering a sense of community. Memories of the event will linger, inspiring participants in their coding endeavors. Congratulations to all who took on the challenge and showcased their coding prowess. "Hello World! 3.0" will be remembered as a day of triumph, passion, and excellence in the coding world. Until the next adventure awaits, keep coding, problem-solving, and conquering new frontiers!

IEEEXTREME 17.0 TRAINING SESSION

IEEE CS FSS SBC and IEEE CS ENIS SBC successfully conducted their inaugural training session for IEEEXTREME 17.0. Given the influx of new students and Computer Science (CS) members, there was a clear demand for an introductory session, as many participants lacked a solid understanding of competitive programming fundamentals and best practices. The session concluded with the new students feeling more at ease as they prepare for their first-ever Problem Solving Competition.

IEEE DAY BOOTCAMP

In celebration of IEEE Day, IEEE ENIS SB orchestrated a dynamic bootcamp, featuring an array of sessions in collaboration with diverse chapters. This comprehensive event, blending both humanitarian and technical workshops, aimed to provide a multifaceted learning experience: IEEE Computer Society ENIS Student Chapter, in collaboration with IEEE Cyber Security Enis Unit, hosted an informative session titled 'Introduction to Cyber Security'.

IEEEXTREME 17.0 FSS X ENIS

IEEE CS ENIS SBC and IEEE CS FSS SBC joined forces for the collaborative IEEEXTREME 17.0 programming competition, leveraging the combined strengths of both university groups. The teamwork resulted in a vibrant exchange of talents and ideas, fostering creative problem-solving and expanding the participants' networks. Before the competition, students bonded over enjoyable activities and a shared meal. Despite challenges during problem-solving, many students achieved commendable rankings. This successful collaboration not only enhanced coding skills but also fostered camaraderie and knowledge exchange, aligning with IEEE's core principles of innovation and technological advancement.

TRAINEE PROGRAM SESSION

The IEEE Computer Society ENIS SBC has decided to leave its legacy in safe hands. To this end, the ExCom’s Chairman has prepared and presented some of the basics of event organization. With their term almost at an end and with a strong belief in continuity, chapter has curated a short trainee program in order to allow following this session that will include tasks that will aid these members to learn what officers do.

WEB DEVELOPMENT WORKSHOP

The IEEE ENIS Computer Society Chapter is partnering with the local Microsoft Tech Club to introduce front-end development to students school-wide. The interactive sessions have garnered excitement, and the chapter is committed to organizing more in the future. The Organizing Committee ensures seamless continuity, and participating students appreciate the value of the sessions for their academic projects.

IEEE CS TECHX TUNISIA SECTION

TechX is an IEEE CS event designed for students and young professionals. TechX also emphasises leadership development and soft skills, providing sessions to help students become effective leaders and prepare for interviews. The IEEE ENIS CS SBC has applied for the TechX initiative and has been accepted to organise the event under the name of "IEEE CS TechX Tunisia Section". The event will include various workshops with the crown jewel of the event being a hackathon to be discovered the day of the event. This event is a pride to the ENIS SB and is a great last event for this year's ExCom. Only time will tell what experiences await you during TechX!

Steps in Cyber Security

IEEE CS JU 🇯🇴 and IEEE Computer Society ENIS Student Chapter successfully wrapped up their collaborative event, "Steps in Cyber Security," featuring Eng. Murad Ghazzawi. The online session, held on December 8, 2023, covered key Cyber security topics, including an introduction, fundamentals, starting a career, and top opportunities. Participants joined via a form shared on social media and email. The event aimed to blend fun with insightful knowledge.

HOUR OF CODE

The IEEE CS ENIS SBC takes the opportunity of the Hour of Code global event to put into place a set of low code/no code programming games for primary school kids likely using Scratch and other alternatives. A nearby school with only a couple of computers within the IT classroom is a solid target to bring technology to these otherwise unfortunate students. The chapter deems it necessary to bring the world of Computer Engineering to these kids in order to broaden their ambitions.

IEEE ENIS CS SBC

The IEEE Computer Society has built a strong reputation for its advocacy of computer science and engineering. At the National School of Engineering of Sfax, the Enis Student Branch Chapter was founded on January 27, 2010, with a clear mission: to equip its members with vital Information Technology knowledge, thus enhancing their career prospects and honing their abilities. By organizing enriching programs and fostering valuable networking connections, the chapter empowers ambitious individuals to thrive amidst the constantly changing technology domain.
